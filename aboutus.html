WrestleMania is the biggest annual professional wrestling event produced by WWE (World Wrestling Entertainment) and is often regarded as the "Super Bowl of professional wrestling."
Created by WWE owner Vince McMahon, the first WrestleMania took place on March 31, 1985,
at Madison Square Garden in New York City, helping to elevate WWE into mainstream entertainment with celebrity appearances like Muhammad Ali,
Cyndi Lauper, and Mr. T. Over the years, it has grown into a global phenomenon, attracting fans worldwide. The event features high-profile main event matches,
championship bouts, and legendary moments, such as The Undertaker’s legendary undefeated streak (21-0) before losing at WrestleMania 30.
Some of the most iconic matches include Hulk Hogan bodyslamming Andre the Giant at WrestleMania 3, Shawn Michaels vs. The Undertaker at WrestleMania 25,
and the intense rivalry between Stone Cold Steve Austin and The Rock across multiple WrestleManias. WrestleMania also includes celebrity appearances, a Hall of Fame induction ceremony,
and has expanded to a two-night event in recent years. It is one of WWE’s biggest financial successes,
selling out stadiums worldwide and influencing pop culture through video games, documentaries, and TV specials.
WrestleMania 40 (2024), held at Lincoln Financial Field in Philadelphia, featured marquee matches like Roman Reigns vs.
Cody Rhodes for the Undisputed WWE Universal Championship and Seth Rollins vs. Drew McIntyre.
As WWE continues to evolve, WrestleMania remains the pinnacle of professional wrestling, bringing together history,
entertainment, and athleticism on the grandest stage of them all.
